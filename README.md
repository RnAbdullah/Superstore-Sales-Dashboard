# 📊 Superstore Sales Performance Dashboard  
Interactive dashboard analyzing sales, profitability, and discount impact.

## 📌 Project Background  
This project was developed as part of building practical skills in data analysis, with a focus on applying structured business thinking to real-world datasets.  

The project focuses on analyzing retail performance data to understand key drivers of sales and profitability, and evaluate the impact of discounting on business performance.  

---

## 📊 Project Overview  
An interactive Power BI dashboard built using the Superstore dataset to analyze sales, profit, and performance across regions, product categories, and customer segments.  

The dashboard allows users to explore trends, compare performance, and identify areas for improvement through dynamic filtering.  

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used  
* Power BI Desktop (Data visualization & dashboard design)  
* Power Query (Data cleaning and transformation)  
* DAX (Calculated measures: Total Sales, Total Profit, Profit Margin, Total Orders)  

---

## 🔍 Analysis Process  
* Cleaned and prepared the dataset for analysis  
* Built calculated measures (Sales, Profit, Profit Margin, Orders)  
* Designed KPI cards to track overall performance  
* Created interactive visuals to analyze trends and relationships  
* Used tooltips to provide detailed product-level insights  

---

## 📈 Key Features  

**KPI cards for:**  
• Total Sales (706K+)  
• Total Profit (91K+)  
• Profit Margin (13%)  
• Total Orders (3K+)  

**Additional features:**  
• Sales trend analysis across months  
• Profit comparison by category  
• Top-performing products based on profit  
• Discount vs Profit analysis  
• Regional performance breakdown (Sales & Profit)  
• Interactive filters (Year, Region, Segment)

---

## 💡 Key Insights  
* Sales increase toward the end of the year, peaking in November (highest monthly sales), indicating strong seasonality  
* Technology is the most profitable category, while Furniture has significantly lower margins  
* Higher discount levels (above ~40–50%) are strongly associated with reduced or even negative profitability  
* Furniture’s low profitability is likely driven by heavier discounting  
* The West region leads in both sales and profit, while the Central region underperforms relative to its sales volume  

---

## 💼 Business Recommendations  
* Focus on high-margin categories such as Technology to improve profitability  
* Reduce excessive discounting in low-margin categories like Furniture (e.g., keep below ~30%)  
* Implement controls on discount levels, as deep discounts are often linked to losses  
* Align marketing and sales efforts with peak demand periods toward year-end  
* Improve performance in underperforming regions by reviewing pricing and cost structure
